Vision Express

One of the first e-commerce custom solution designed as a responsive web design from the ground up. Launched as a Minimum Viable Product and developed into a customer’s digital experience system.

My role was UX research coordination: usability studies with eye tracking and in-depth interview elements—done as a benchmark and with an Axure prototype. The research was facilitated by external contractors at a usability lab.

We did a field studies and stores, to understand how visits are managed by staff so we could design a better solution for their needs and context of their everyday work.

I was also directing UX design (workshops with the client and internal teams, oversight of web production with RWD principles) and I have created an interactive prototype in Axure.

The outcomes:

  • e-commerce that was easy to use, with a conversion rate or 5% (processed purchases),
  • application to manage in-store visits across 160 brick and mortar location across Poland.

This project was launched in 2012, and although a different software house supports the project right now, the project still stands and is still in use.

FROØPT

FRØPTs are custom fronts for IKEA kitchen cabinets. The challenge was to create an e-commerce and communication website.

I was responsible for the strategy for the product, UX design direction, UI design, usability research, and web production.

Due to the complexity of available sizes of the cabinets, no existing e-commerce solution could have been used.

So… back to sketches for me, and into some in-depth interviews with existing clients…

Although the business side was focused, the configurator was not easy to design. The number of options, the order of the steps and choices—a lot of elements were interdependent.

After the launch, the first orders happened in the first weeks, and FRØPT clients expressed their satisfaction with calm and simple communication as well as an intuitive flow—although, I must admit, that I see a lot of things that can be improved, when the right time comes.

The project still evolves, and the FRØPT business and co-founders were recognised by furniture and interior design industries and had received awards and stories about custom, high-quality cabinets, where published in many magazines.

The back office helps to organise the sales and delivery process.

miastologia

A 3-year long project to bring well-designed apartments as affordable housing, without losing the quality of the interior and comfort of living. Easier said than done—and done.

I was responsible for digital products:

  • Communication website—contrary to competition, we decided to put a fully transparent offer online, with easy to understand pricing model and no hidden costs. There were a lot of pieces of information to be included and a lot of reading for the people, but the availability and simplicity of the website enabled people to go through every detail that mattered to them.
  • Back office—the goal was to bring the sales process online, and to everything internally (without hiring external real estate agents). The flows were complicated due to a lot of legislation related to the real estate market.
  • Apartment configurator—an app that allowed people to make a reservation, select options for topology and fit-out and prepare for notary agreement. Apartments were modular, so one bigger apartment (M-4) took the place of two smaller ones (M-2)—it was a peoples choice, which of those will be purchased and eventually build.

Apart from digital, I was doing service design, like the experience with showroom, sales flow, and even the wayfinding and navigation in the space.

My responsibility was also to hire, oversight and direct work of a digital agency (later I hired a full stack developer internally) and to co-operate with branding agency.

During the 3 years, we had interview in-person more than 200 people who visited our showroom (build in 1:1 scale M-2 apartment)—this impacted our understanding of peoples decision process and needs significantly. It also helped to improve the ergonomics of the flats.

The research activities included competitive reports, field observations and a ton of desk research. As for the digital products, we had used analytics and interactions recording to identify struggles people might have had with the website and apps.

As the miastologia team was multidisciplinary, everyone has worn many hats, so I also did illustrations and posters.

The primary outcomes for the project were:

  • Most people who are not moving in or renting their apartments are satisfied with their experience and investment.
  • People bought their apartments online—that was a significant change compared to the market status quo.
  • The back office investment broke even after the first location sold out.
  • A complicated product structure did not negatively impact the experiences of configuration the apartments.
  • miastologia is now perceived as an innovator at the real estate market—after only 3 years of operations.
